Introduction

Current Process Flow of Manufacturing Cannabis Edibles

Canopy Growth Corporation is a leading cannabis company that manufactures a wide range of products, including cannabis edibles. The current process flow for manufacturing cannabis edibles at Canopy Growth Corporation is outlined below:

  • Raw Material Sourcing: Procurement of high-quality cannabis and other ingredients.
  • Quality Check: All raw materials undergo rigorous quality checks.
  • Material Storage: Storage of quality-approved materials.
  • Production Planning: Planning the production schedule and resource allocation.
  • Manufacturing: Actual production of cannabis edibles.
  • Quality Assurance: Quality checks for the finished products.
  • Packaging: Packaging the quality-approved edibles.
  • Storage: Storing the packaged products in a controlled environment.
  • Distribution: Distributing the products to various retail outlets.
  • Retail: The final products are sold to consumers.

Shortcomings:

  • Lack of automation in quality checks.
  • Inefficient resource allocation during production planning.
  • Limited storage capacity affecting production scale.

o elucidate the supply-demand dynamics in the context of DOMESTIC sales, a causal loop diagram was developed. This diagram includes a minimum of 10 entities, which represent various factors influencing the supply and demand of Canopy Growth Corporation's cannabis edibles in the domestic market. The logic behind this diagram is to illustrate how changes in factors such as production capacity, pricing, and consumer preferences can affect the overall supply and demand equilibrium. The diagram captures both reinforcing and balancing feedback loops that play a crucial role in shaping market dynamics (Sterman, 2000).

Below is the causal loop diagram for the supply-demand model with DOMESTIC sales:

The causal loop diagram for DOMESTIC sales provides insights into the interdependencies between various factors influencing the supply and demand of Canopy Growth Corporation's cannabis edibles in the domestic market (Sterman, 2000). For instance, an increase in production capacity might lead to a decrease in product price, which in turn could boost consumer demand. Conversely, a surge in consumer preferences for a particular product variant might necessitate adjustments in production schedules (Leafly, 2023). The reinforcing loops indicate factors that amplify changes, while the balancing loops show factors that counteract or stabilize changes. Understanding these dynamics helps in making informed decisions related to production, pricing, and marketing strategies (Cannabis Business Times, 2023).
