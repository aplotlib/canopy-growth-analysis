Introduction

Current Process Flow of Manufacturing Cannabis Edibles

Canopy Growth Corporation is a leading cannabis company that manufactures a wide range of products, including cannabis edibles. The current process flow for manufacturing cannabis edibles at Canopy Growth Corporation is outlined below:

  • Raw Material Sourcing: Procurement of high-quality cannabis and other ingredients.
  • Quality Check: All raw materials undergo rigorous quality checks.
  • Material Storage: Storage of quality-approved materials.
  • Production Planning: Planning the production schedule and resource allocation.
  • Manufacturing: Actual production of cannabis edibles.
  • Quality Assurance: Quality checks for the finished products.
  • Packaging: Packaging the quality-approved edibles.
  • Storage: Storing the packaged products in a controlled environment.
  • Distribution: Distributing the products to various retail outlets.
  • Retail: The final products are sold to consumers.

Shortcomings:

  • Lack of automation in quality checks.
  • Inefficient resource allocation during production planning.
  • Limited storage capacity affecting production scale.

2a. Causal Loop Diagram for DOMESTIC Sales

To elucidate the supply-demand dynamics in the context of DOMESTIC sales, here a causal loop diagram is presented. This diagram encompasses a minimum of 10 entities, representing various factors that influence the supply and demand of Canopy Growth Corporation's cannabis edibles in the domestic market.

The primary purpose of this diagram is to illustrate the interplay of factors such as production capacity, pricing, and consumer preferences, and how they impact the overall supply and demand equilibrium. Importantly, the diagram captures both reinforcing and balancing feedback loops, which are pivotal in shaping market dynamics (Sterman, 2000).

The causal loop diagram offers insights into the intricate interdependencies between various factors influencing the domestic market's supply and demand for Canopy Growth Corporation's cannabis edibles (Sterman, 2000). For example, an uptick in production capacity might lead to a price reduction, potentially boosting consumer demand. On the flip side, a rise in consumer preference for a specific product variant could necessitate production adjustments (Leafly, 2023). Reinforcing loops highlight factors that amplify changes, while balancing loops indicate factors that counteract or stabilize these changes. Grasping these dynamics is instrumental in making informed decisions related to production, pricing, and marketing strategies (Cannabis Business Times, 2023).

Operation Analysis

1a. Operational Processes

Manufacturing

The cannabis-infused beverages are produced using high-quality cannabis extracts combined with natural flavors. The process involves sourcing raw materials, extraction, blending, and quality checks to ensure consistent potency and taste (Canopy Growth, 2020, November 5).

Shipping

Beverages are packaged in tamper-evident, child-resistant containers. They're shipped using trusted carriers with real-time tracking to ensure timely and safe delivery (Future Market Insights, 2022, October).

Warehousing

Products are stored in climate-controlled warehouses to maintain freshness. An advanced inventory management system tracks product batches and expiration dates (Rajgopal, n.d.).

Distributions

The beverages are distributed through licensed dispensaries, online platforms, and partner cafes (Thompson-Richards, A., 2023, March 29).

Vendors and Suppliers

Canopy sources cannabis from licensed growers who adhere to organic farming practices. Natural flavor extracts are procured from trusted suppliers (Universal Teacher Publications, n.d.).

Retailing

Apart from dispensaries, Canopy has an online platform that offers home delivery in select areas. Canopy also collaborates with cafes to offer cannabis beverages as a menu item (CFI Team, 2020, June 25; Updated 2023, May 11).
